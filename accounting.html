My Tentative Career Plan

I will graduate from Brigham Young University with a Master's in Accountancy in the spring of 2026. I will most likely follow the Professional Stem track. I hope to get some public accounting experience for a few years - perhaps at one of the Big Four companies- after I graduate to get some exposure to different businesses and industries and to expand my network. I am also interested in other areas of business, such as management consulting. Although I'm not sure where exactly I'll end up, I know accounting is an excellent foundation for a business career and I am excited to see where it takes me.

Here are some things I have to offer in my future business career:

  • A unique educational background. I majored in Economics, but took many of the premed courses, including biology, physics, and chemistry. These classes taught me how to think analytically and to work hard.

  • Extensive volunteer experience. I spent several hundred hours volunteering throughout my undergraduate years at hospitals, care centers, and tutoring centers. I was able to serve many different populations and learn about people with stories different from my own. The knowledge and skills I gained through these experiences will help me to have positive interactions with future customers, clients, and co-workers.

  • Organizational and problem-solving skills. I consider myself to be a pretty organized and detailed person. My favorite subject has always been math and I especially loved organic chemistry during college. I enjoy learning formulas and rules and applying them to problems to find solutions.
